Secretive Firms From U.K. to Singapore Win $200M in Tenders From Uzbek State Giant

A major Uzbek state mining company awarded hundreds of millions of dollars in tenders to companies in the U.K., Georgia, and Singapore. Reporters found they have connections to an Uzbek ping-pong official, while some have unclear ownership information or received contracts despite company filings indicating they were inactive.

Reported by
Ilya Lozovsky OCCRP

Key Players
Vladimir Putin
President of Russia

A Russian politician and former intelligence officer who has served as President of Russia since 2012, having previously served from 2000 to 2008. Putin also served as Prime Minister of Russia from 1999 to 2000 and again from 2008 to 2012.

Emmanuel Macron
President of France

A French politician who has served as President of France and Co-Prince of Andorra since 2017. He served as Minister of Economics and Finance under President François Hollande from 2014 to 2016. He has been a member of Renaissance since founding the party in 2016.

Péter Magyar
Member of the European Parliament

A Hungarian politician and lawyer who is the president of the Tisza Party. He led the party to victory in the 2026 parliamentary election and is expected to become the next prime minister of Hungary. Magyar served as a member of the European Parliament from 2024 to 2026.

Key Findings
  • A state mining giant in Uzbekistan awarded more than $200 million in tenders to 10 foreign firms in multiple countries that have connections to an Uzbek ping-pong official.
  • Two of the companies made a flurry of changes to their respective corporate structures after OCCRP and Finance Uncovered inquired about their ownership.
  • Several of the companies that won tenders are, on paper at least, inactive.
  • Millions of dollars in contracts between the Uzbek company and two U.K.-incorporated tender winners feature signatures of individuals who say they never signed the documents.
