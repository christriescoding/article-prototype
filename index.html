Secretive Firms From U.K. to Singapore Win $200M in Tenders From Uzbek State Giant

A major Uzbek state mining company awarded hundreds of millions of dollars in tenders to companies in the U.K., Georgia, and Singapore. Reporters found they have connections to an Uzbek ping-pong official, while some have unclear ownership information or received contracts despite company filings indicating they were inactive.

Key Findings
  • A state mining giant in Uzbekistan awarded more than $200 million in tenders to 10 foreign firms in multiple countries that have connections to an Uzbek ping-pong official.
  • Two of the companies made a flurry of changes to their respective corporate structures after OCCRP and Finance Uncovered inquired about their ownership.
  • Several of the companies that won tenders are, on paper at least, inactive.
  • Millions of dollars in contracts between the Uzbek company and two U.K.-incorporated tender winners feature signatures of individuals who say they never signed the documents.
February 4, 2025
|
11 min read

Over the past three years, two U.K. companies with no prior record in the mining industry have won tens of millions of dollars in Uzbek state procurement tenders connected with a mining behemoth known as a "crown jewel" of Uzbekistan's economy.

One was owned, on paper, by a septuagenarian British bookkeeper with no evident ties to Central Asia; the other, by a U.K. corporate services provider that for years managed corporate structures that shielded their true ownership from public view.

Following inquiries from reporters in late 2023, the U.K. firms retroactively installed Khvan and the Colombian man, Felipe Guerrero, as "persons of significant control" according to filings from the U.K. companies registry. In a chaotic sequence of filings, Khvan was added as their owner.

Backdated to 2018, only to be replaced weeks later by Guerrero, whose control was backdated to that same year. Furthermore, the two U.K. firms declared themselves as "dormant" during reporting periods in which they were awarded tens of millions of dollars in AMMC tenders and, according to commercially available import-export records, sent numerous shipments of equipment to AMMC.
